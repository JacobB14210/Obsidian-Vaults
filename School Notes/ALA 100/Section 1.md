# What is Design?
- Designo: To mark out and define
- Until Renaissance there was no such thing as design
- Design: Plan of action to address a specific problem or need
	- Purpose (function) + significance (form + meaning)
- Great designers constantly seek answers to life's questions and problems
	- They always are asking Why? How? What?
- Great designers always create meaningful connections
- Great designers always push the edge
## Why Has Design Become so Important?
- Old economy was focused on job specific education and a guaranteed life employment
- New economy is a rapidly changing world
	- Traditional disciplinary boundaries are being redefined and re-conceptualized
	- Design has become essential
## What are the key Methods Great Designers Have Used?
1. Heightened awareness of the world around them
2. Engage yourself in challenging problems
3. Design is an iterative process of intellectual and creative discovery
4. Collaborate with dedicated individuals who love their work
5. Develop new ways of perceiving and thinking
## What is Good and Bad Design?
- Good Design
	- Innovative: Improves and changes our lives
	- Functional and user orientated: It works and empowers us
	- Simple and clear: You don't need to read directions
	- Responsible: Respects environment and is sustainable
	- Honest: Economy of gesture, a simplicity and elegance
	- Intuitive: Easy to understand
	- Durable: Not disposable and passed on to others
	- Good business: Widely valued, appreciated and makes money
- Bad Design
	- Reflects status quo: Preconceived and is not innovative
	- Not functional: We can't use it
	- Complicated and confusing
	- Irresponsible: Does not solve the problem
	- Dishonest: It looks cool, but doesn't work and falls apart
	- Produced last minute
	- Disposable: Not sustainable or environmentally friendly
	- Bad business: Short term economic gain
## What Design Issues are Affecting our Lives?

- Increased design collaboration is replacing single heroic acts of design

# The Design Process
- Design process: Defined as a problem specific iterative method, engaged by a particular team f design collaborators to explore, understand and search for a resolution to a problematic situation and need
- Personal knowledge system
	- Stance guides tools guides experiences
	- Experiences informs tools informs stance
- Iterative design process
	- Ideation: Identify real world problem
	- Visualization: Ideas and solutions
	- Prototyping: Test out ideas and evaluate
	- Implementation: Produce, present, and implement tangible solutions to real world problems
- Iterative design process framework
	- Question - solve - test - reflect - refine
- Fail quickly and often
- Respect constraints
	- Ability to identify and work within these constraints
	- Each problem have there own unique constraints
- Danger, the fallacy of originality
	- Design is not about self expression
	- Not dependent on originality
	- Is self discipline and takes endless hours of dedicated work
- Common design process faults
	- Following you pre-conceptions versus solving problems
	- Blindly devoted to your first creations
	- Lack of self confidence
	- Unwillingness to trust the process
	- Unwilling to collaborate
- Five dysfunctions of collaborative design team
	- Absence of trust
	- Fear of conflict
	- Lack of passionate commitment
	- Failure to embrace accountability
	- Failure to pay attention to results
